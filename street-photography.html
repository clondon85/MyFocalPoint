Street Photography

Chase the decisive moment. Find the human element.

About this course
Street photography rewards the photographer who shows up with their eyes open.

There is no controlled environment in street photography. No direction, no second chances, no safety net. It's you, whatever is in front of you, and the fraction of a second in which a picture either exists or doesn't. That's what makes it demanding, and what makes a good street photograph worth more than a technically perfect studio shot.

This course works through the practical and psychological sides of shooting on the street. Through video lessons, written content, and interactive exercises, you'll build the confidence to work in public spaces, the technical fluency to keep up with fast-moving scenes, and the eye to recognize when something worth photographing is happening before it's gone.

Street photography has a deep history and a lot of noise around it. This course cuts through the mythology and focuses on what actually helps you make better photographs in the real world.

What you'll cover
Inside the course.
Working in public spaces
How to approach strangers, navigate public spaces with confidence, and handle the discomfort that comes with photographing people who haven't asked to be photographed.
Shooting fast and staying ready
Camera settings, gear considerations, and shooting habits that let you react quickly. How to work in changing light without losing the shot while you're adjusting.
The decisive moment
What the decisive moment actually means in practice, and how to train your eye to anticipate it. Pre-visualization, patience, and knowing when to press the shutter.
Finding your own voice
Street photography has strong aesthetic traditions. This section is about developing a point of view that's yours: what you're drawn to, what you notice, and why that matters.
